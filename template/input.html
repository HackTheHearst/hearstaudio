Why is this important?

Wika is Tagalog for culture - where history, language, and antiquities merge in mutual understanding. One intersection is through the eyes of anthropologist, Dr.David P. Barrows who studied tribal factions in the Philippines prior to pre-colonial rule. He studied the natives in their habitus. An educator born in Chicago in 1874, his interest first started in the life and customs of the American Indians leading him to "spend almost every summer during 1890â€“1899 researching the tribes of Southern California and the Colorado desert. Later, he worked as Chief of the Bureau of Non-Christian Tribes of the Philippine Islands recording the ethnography of unknown regions of the islands for military reconnaisance. In 1903, as general superintendent of education for the Islands, he led the total reorganization of the education system establishing English language learning in schools. He also wrote The History of the Philippines and later became President of the University. His expertise was crucial in shaping public policy on human rights especially in the Senate Hearings on the Philippines. His notes over nine years of study of the islands are still accesible through archives today. His collection can be accesed through this app along with the many other collectors who have contributed to the Hearst over the years.

Wika curates the collection so children K-12, researchers and heritage language learners will be able to access anywhere in the world then they will be able to add their own audio elements each photo and learn from each other.
